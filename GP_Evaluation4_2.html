Linear Modeling Results - Positive

The lm() function in R creates a regression model with the given data, and the summary functions reviews the weights and performance measures of the model. The residuals allows us to measure and analyze the difference between prediction and actual results of the linear model. Residuals, along with other factors such as the p-value, are observed and tested to see how well the single-variable linear regression model fits the data.

Weather is another factor that logically seems like a big factor to the popularity of golf. Not only is it an outside sport, but golf courses rely on lots of water, and players mostly want to experience a nice weather as they play 18 holes, so its assumed that there is a relationship between temperature and golf popularity. The p value of the our linear model for both boys and girls was below 0.05, which means that changes in temperature correlate to changes in the amount of golf players. However, the scatterplot with the regression line does not look fitting, as we can visually see some high residuals.

Based on how far we analyzed the reisuals and linear regression line, we can say that the temperature in each state is a significant factor that affects golf popularity within that area. However, the problem is that a linear regression model shouldnt be appropriate for anything weather-related, because it is not a linear relationship - there is a middle, preferred temperature, while extremes are unfavored. Therefore, we are very restricted by only doing a linear regression model, and we can just barely discern temperature as a significant factor in golf popularity.
